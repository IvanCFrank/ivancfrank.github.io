Dr. Ivan C. Frank

Dr. Ivan Frank is a veteran educator with more than sixty years of teaching experience, both in the United States and Israel. He lived on a kibbutz in Israel in the 1950s, later with his family from 1968 to 1972 in the second urban collective in Israeli history, and returned again to Israel from 1976 thru 1982.

Dr. Ivan C. Frank is a noted American educator and expert on high-risk youth. He has taught History, Philosophy, and Education courses in high schools, colleges, universities, and youth centers in the United States and Israel for over forty years. He was a Fulbright Fellow in the summer of 1998, studying in Israel and Jordan. Following that period of scholarship, he led workshops for Social Studies teachers at the conventions of the National Council of Social Studies on Israeli and Jordanian history, their peace agreement, and their economic, social, and cultural relationships.

He lived with his family in a kibbutz from 1977 to 1982, during which time he was coordinator of the Education Department of the Regional College of the Negev and taught at Ben Gurion University. He worked with high-risk youth in Pittsburgh from 1982 to 1990 as a teacher, coordinator of an alternative educational program, psychometrist, and educational advocate.

Ivan holds a Ph.D. in International Education from the University of Pittsburgh, International Education Development Program (IEDP), with special emphasis on educational and social movements. While living in Israel, he wrote his dissertation on at-risk children, returned to Pittsburgh to defend his research and received his doctorate in 1981. He also holds a M.A. in Modern European History (1972) and B.A. in Education (1963), with undergraduate majors in History and Social Sciences from the University of Pittsburgh.

Dr. Frank has taught at Ben Gurion University and the Regional College of the Negev, both in Israel. He has also worked for the Histradrut in Israel, training African and Asian immigrants in the development of labor and cooperative movements, and organized educational development seminars on kibbutz life for Kibbutz Nachal Oz. He has taught courses on Israeli history, Israeli current events, and the development of socialism in Israel for the Osher Program at the University of Pittsburgh and Carnegie Mellon University.

Dr. Frank has written nine books, five on the nation of Israel, including his political memoir; two which address kibbutz ideology and its relevance to the rehabilitation of at-risk children; another analyzes the democratic socialist period of 1880-1935 (2011); and a fourth surveys modern Israeli history with a view to the future (2008). His book, Children in Chaos (Praeger Publishing, 1992) is included in the permanent collection of the University of Pittsburgh and Carnegie Library in New York City.

Dr. Frank is a member of Ameinu ("Our People"), a progressive Labor Zionist movement, and member of J Street, the pre-eminent U.S.-based pro-Israel / pro-peace advocacy group. He lives with his wife, Malke, in Pittsburgh.
